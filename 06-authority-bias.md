# Authority Bias

## Category
Human Biases

## Description
Authority Bias is the tendency to attribute greater accuracy to the opinion of an authority figure and be more influenced by that opinion. This prompt helps identify and challenge claims based on authority rather than evidence.

## Prompt
Use this prompt to evaluate claims made by authority figures:

**Authority Bias Evaluation:**
1. What specific claim is being made?
2. Who is making the claim and what makes them an authority?
3. What is the evidence supporting the claim (separate from the authority's credentials)?
4. Has this claim been verified by other independent sources?
5. What potential conflicts of interest might the authority have?
6. How would you evaluate this claim if it came from a non-authority source?
7. What would change your mind about this claim?

## Usage
- Best Used For: Evaluating expert opinions, reviewing recommendations, assessing advice
- Complementary Skills: Evidence Evaluation, Assumption Analysis, Perspective Understanding
- Skill Type: Critical Thinking Technique
