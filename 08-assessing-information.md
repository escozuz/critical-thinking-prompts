# Assessing Information Quality: Evaluate Reliability, Relevance, and Validity

## Category
AI Critical Thinking Techniques

## Description
This prompt provides a structured framework for evaluating the quality and reliability of information sources. It helps you assess whether information is credible, relevant, and valid before using it for decision-making.

## Prompt
**Information Assessment Framework:**

Use this framework to evaluate any source of information:

1. **Reliability Assessment:**
2.    - Is the source credible and well-established?
      -    - What is the author's expertise and track record?
           -    - Are sources cited and verifiable?
                -    - Does the information come from primary or secondary sources?
                     -    - What is the potential bias of the source?
                      
                          - 2. **Relevance Evaluation:**
                            3.    - How directly does this information address your question?
                                  -    - Is it current and up-to-date?
                                       -    - Does it apply to your specific context?
                                            -    - Are there important contextual details missing?
                                             
                                                 - 3. **Validity Checking:**
                                                   4.    - Can you verify this information from multiple sources?
                                                         -    - Are claims supported by evidence?
                                                              -    - Have experts in the field validated this?
                                                                   -    - What is the margin of error or uncertainty?
                                                                        -    - Are alternative explanations addressed?
                                                                         
                                                                             - 4. **Critical Questions:**
                                                                               5.    - What assumptions underlie this information?
                                                                                     -    - What would disprove this claim?
                                                                                          -    - Who benefits from this information being true?
                                                                                               -    - What information might be missing or hidden?
                                                                                                
                                                                                                    - ## Usage
                                                                                                    - - Best Used For: Research evaluation, fact-checking, source assessment
                                                                                                      - - Complementary Skills: Evidence Evaluation, Authority Bias, Understanding Perspectives
                                                                                                        - - Skill Type: Critical Thinking Technique
